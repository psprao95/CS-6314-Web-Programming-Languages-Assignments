London

London is the capital city of England. It is the most populous city in the United Kingdom, with a metropolitan area of 13 million inhabitants. Standing on the river Thames, London has been a major settlement for two millenia, its history going back to its founding by the romans, who named it Londinium.

Paris

Paris is the capital and the most popular city of France. It has an area of 105 km2 (41 mi2) and a population of 2,229,631 in 2013 within the city limits. Paris was founded in the 3rd century BC by a celtic people known as the Parisii, who gave the city its name. By the 12th century, Paris was the largest city in the western world, a prosperous trading center, and the home of the University of Paris,one of the oldest universities in history. In the 17th century it was the capital of Europe. In the 18th century, it ws the centerstage of the French revolution, and was an important center of finance, commerce, fashion, science and arts, a position it still retains today.

Tokyo

Tokyo, officially Tokyo is one of the 47 prefectures of Japan, and is both the capital and the most populous city of Japan. The greater tokyo area is one of the most populous areas in the world.
